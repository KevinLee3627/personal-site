## things i've learned

my first time ever baking something on my own. used the recipe from [Natasha's Kitchen](https://natashaskitchen.com/baklava-recipe/) and learned many lessons about baking...

1. baking is fun!
2. having the right equipment can help a lot
3. timing is important - phyllo dough apparently needs to be defrosted the day before you actually use it, according to Natasha and her Kitchen
4. it's good to live in a walkable city with multiple grocery stores that sell things like frozen phyllo dough
5. it's hard to measure exactly 1/2 of a cup of honey - how could i possibly measure a 1/2 cup of honey accurately? when you pour honey into a measure cup and then into a pot, how much honey gets left behind on the inside walls of the measuring cup? it would be better if the recipe provided the number of grams of honey you need so I can weigh it on the scale and put it in the final vessel from the get-go, instead of trying (and failing) for a 100% transfer rate from measuring cup to pot. how do professionals do it? let's suppose you have some kind of mini-scraper to scrape out the inside of your measuring cup - how do you ensure that you get all of the honey out of the corners of the measuring cup, and how can you really get everything off the scraper itself? if you use some other tool to get the remaning honey off of the mini-scraper, how do you get the little bits of honey off of _that_ tool? **who watches the watchmen**?
6. i might have some kind of mental illness

overall, it was not too bad to make - a little tedious if nothing else, but nothing too mechanically difficult! and if you gaze long into the abyss of your neuroticism, the abyss stares back. and then urges you to purchase a kitchen scale with one-tenth gram accuracy
